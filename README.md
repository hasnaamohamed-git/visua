# Climate Impact on Egyptian Agriculture and Business

**A Data Visualization Dashboard (2015–2024)**

An interactive storytelling dashboard analyzing how climate and weather changes over the past decade have affected agriculture, business opportunities, and urban planning in Egypt, with a special focus on Alexandria.

![Dashboard Overview](Screenshot%20(648).png)

---

## 📋 Project Overview

This project explores the relationship between climate variables (temperature, precipitation, soil moisture, humidity) and their impact on Egyptian agriculture and business sectors. It was developed as part of a **Data Visualization** course in the **Data Science** program.

The dashboard tells a compelling story across four main analytical chapters:
- **Nile Soil** – Soil fertility and stability
- **Temperature Last Decade** – Temperature trends and human impact
- **Rainy Alexandria** – Precipitation patterns and coastal risks
- **Alex Weather Tour** – Detailed case study of Alexandria

---

## 🎯 Main Message

> **Climate change in Egypt is not abstract — it is actively reshaping where to farm, where to invest, and how coastal cities must adapt.**

## 🔍 Key Insights

- **Soil Stability**: Giza shows high variance (21.4%), while Kafr El Sheikh and Dakahlia are much more stable.
- **Human Impact**: Significant temperature drop in 2019–2020 linked to COVID-19 lockdowns.
- **Extreme Rainfall**: Sharp spike in 2020, with Alexandria accounting for **18.2%** of total rainfall.
- **Alexandria’s Potential**: Soil moisture increased by **46%** over the decade, opening new agricultural opportunities.
