A. Story Explanation

This dashboard tells a comprehensive story about how climate and weather changes in Egypt over the past decade (2015-2024) have fundamentally altered agricultural opportunities, business risks, and urban planning considerations, with Alexandria serving as a detailed case study for coastal adaptation strategies.

The narrative unfolds in four connected chapters:

  • Nile Soil (The Beginning): Overview of soil fertility variance and moisture across Egyptian governorates, revealing ideal zones for agricultural investment versus unstable areas.
  • Temperature Last Decade (The Middle): Analysis of temperature trends over the decade, with particular focus on the 2019-2020 drop due to COVID-19 pandemic impact on industrial activity, proving significant human impact on environmental pollution.
  • Rainy Alexandria (Specific Analysis): Examination of precipitation patterns, focusing on the sharp 2020 increase, especially in coastal governorates like Alexandria (18.20% of total rainfall).
  • Alex Weather Tour (The End): Detailed Alexandria case study, combining temperature, humidity, soil moisture, and rainfall to provide actionable business and urban planning recommendations.

Main Message: Climate change in Egypt is not an abstract concept - it is reshaping where to farm, where to invest, and how coastal cities must prepare for increased risks. Businesses and governments must act now based on this data.

C. Insights & Recommendations

Insight 1: Soil Fertility Variance Creates Opportunities and Risks

Data: Giza has the highest soil moisture variance , while Kafr El Sheikh and Dakahlia have low variance .

Recommendations:

  • For Agricultural Companies: Invest in high-stability areas like Kafr El Sheikh and Dakahlia for reliable crop production. Avoid Giza for long-term agriculture.
  • For Real Estate Developers: Focus on Giza and Greater Cairo for housing, rather than agriculture. However, invest in soil studies to avoid foundation issues.
  • For Government: Create national soil fertility maps to guide land allocation (agriculture vs. housing vs. industry).

Insight 2: COVID-19 Proved Significant Human Impact on Climate

Data: Temperatures dropped sharply in 2019-2020 , coinciding with COVID-19 lockdowns and decreased industrial activity.

Recommendations:

  • For Industrial Companies: Adopt green technologies now to avoid future stricter regulations. Invest in renewable energy (solar/wind) and energy-efficient operations.
  • For Government: Use this data to support environmental policies. Impose carbon taxes or incentives for green practices.
  • For Investors: Shift portfolios toward eco-friendly companies - the green market is growing rapidly.

Insight 3: Sharp Rainfall Increase in 2020, Especially Coastal Areas

Data: Rainfall jumped in 2020, with Alexandria representing 18.20% of total rainfall.

Recommendations:

  • For Coastal Cities (Alexandria, Marsa Matrouh): Urgently invest in flood management infrastructure - drainage systems, sea barriers, and green infrastructure (rain gardens).
  • For Clothing Retailers: Focus on winter/rain wear in Alexandria, and light summer clothing in inland governorates like Sohag.
  • For Insurance Companies: Recalculate risk models. Increase premiums for coastal properties or require flood protection measures.

Insight 4: Alexandria is Transforming - New Agricultural Potential

Data: Alexandria's soil moisture increased from 17.73 (2015) to 25.81 (2024), a 46% rise. Rainfall peaked in 2020 (260.30 mm).

Recommendations:

  • For Local Government: Explore urban agriculture initiatives in Alexandria - rooftop gardens, community green spaces, and coastal greenhouses.
  • For Agricultural Investors: Start pilot projects in Alexandria, especially for high-value crops (organic vegetables, herbs) that thrive in moderate humid climates.
  • For Business Owners: Protect assets against flooding, but also leverage increased fertility for urban agricultural ventures (e.g., farm-to-table restaurants).

Conclusion

This dashboard reveals that climate change in Egypt is not a distant threat - it is happening now, reshaping the economy. The data is clear:

✓ Agriculture: Invest in stable governorates like Kafr El Sheikh, avoid Giza.

✓ Environment: Transition to green models is urgent - COVID-19 proved human impact.

✓ Coastal Areas: Alexandria faces flood risks but also new agricultural opportunities.

✓ Business: Diversification, flexibility, and data-driven planning are keys to survival and prosperity.

Companies and governments that act on these insights now will lead Egypt's future economy. Those who ignore them will face increasing risks and costs.
